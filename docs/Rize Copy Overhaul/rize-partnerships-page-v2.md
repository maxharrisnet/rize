# Partnerships that bring practical technology education into your community.

Rize partners with community organizations, nonprofits, and other trusted groups to deliver workshops, series, and related support that help people build real confidence with technology. In many cases, we also help identify funding opportunities that can make the work possible at little or no cost to the organization or participants.

## Who partnerships are for

Rize works best with organizations that already have trust and relationships in the communities they serve.

### Common partner types
- Nonprofits and charities.
- Community organizations and community-led groups.
- Social service, settlement, and employment organizations.
- Libraries and public-serving community spaces.
- Indigenous-led groups and nations.
- Black-led community organizations.

You bring the community, the trust, and the local knowledge. Rize brings the curriculum, facilitation, technology guidance, and partnership support needed to deliver something genuinely useful.

## What partnership can include

Partnerships can be as light or as deep as the context calls for.

### Core partnership components
- Workshops on digital confidence, digital safety, AI, modern work, and related topics.
- Multi-session series for deeper transformation, including organizational capacity-building programs.
- Tailored delivery for a specific audience, cohort, or community context.
- Optional supporting services such as websites, internal systems, or grant-writing support when the engagement calls for it.

## Funding and affordability

Many organizations want to deliver this kind of training but assume it will be out of reach. In practice, some community-focused digital skills and AI upskilling work can be supported through existing provincial or federal funding streams, depending on the audience, geography, and program goals.

### What Rize can help with
- Identifying relevant funding opportunities.
- Shaping a program to fit the opportunity.
- Supporting application language and documentation where appropriate.
- Delivering the actual program once the work is approved or scoped.

## How it works

### Step 1: Reach out
Share who you serve, what your goals are, and what kind of support you think may help.

### Step 2: We design together
We identify the right format, topics, audience fit, and possible funding path where relevant.

### Step 3: We deliver
Rize handles curriculum, facilitation, and materials, with delivery shaped for your community and context.

### Step 4: You keep the momentum
Participants leave with practical tools and reference material, and organizations can continue with follow-up support, further training, or systems implementation if needed.

## What partners walk away with

- A program shaped to the actual needs of your community.
- Participants who leave with more confidence and usable skills.
- Resources and materials that help learning continue after the session.
- In some engagements, stronger internal systems, clearer workflows, or next-step implementation support.

## Why this model works

People learn best in places where they already feel supported. Community organizations understand their people, their barriers, and their goals. Rize’s role is to bring practical technology education into those spaces in a way that feels useful, respectful, and grounded in real life.
