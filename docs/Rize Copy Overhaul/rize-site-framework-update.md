# Rize Skill Training — Site Framework Update

## How the pieces fit

The site should now work as one connected system rather than separate learning and services branches. The cleanest structure is:

- **Community**
- **Career**
- **Creative**
- **How It Works**
- **About**
- **Contact**

Each audience page can hold the relevant learning Series, services, and automations for that group. That keeps the story simple for visitors: choose the context that fits you, then see the training, implementation, and support options in one place.

---

## Service model

Services should stay in the mix, but they should support the audience pages instead of competing with them. The two strongest service layers are:

- **Websites**.
- **Internal tooling and systems**.

Websites are the front door. Internal tooling is the back office and operational layer. Together, they support the learning Series by helping organizations move from information to implementation.

---

## Recommended page roles

### Community
This page should carry the heaviest mix of content.

It should include:
- A weekly Series for digital confidence and safety.
- A weekly Series for organizational transformation.
- Service offers for websites and internal tooling.
- Automation support for donation workflows, CRM, reporting, and outreach.

This is where your strongest nonprofit and community-partnership value lives.

### Career
This page should stay focused on outcomes and learning.

It should include:
- One clear career upskilling Series.
- Modular sessions that can be customized for workforce or employment partners.
- Very little or no separate service selling.

The page should feel like a program page, not a broad services page.

### Creative
This page can stay lean for now.

It should include:
- One creative business Series.
- Optional tailoring by sector such as music or film.
- Website and launch-support services only where they clearly fit the partner.

If demand is lighter here, keep it simple and easy to maintain.

---

## Service framework

### Websites
Websites should remain a core service across the site, especially for Community and Creative.

The website offer can be framed as:
- Public presence.
- Registration or conversion support.
- Program and event communication.
- Stronger trust and visibility.

### Internal tooling
This should be framed as the operational layer that helps organizations work better day to day.

Examples include:
- Google Workspace setup or cleanup.
- Slack or communication workflows.
- Donation and CRM workflows.
- Reporting and follow-up systems.
- Automation that reduces repetitive admin.

This aligns especially well with nonprofit and community work.

---

## How It Works page

This page becomes the bridge between the audience pages and the service/learning mix.

It should explain:
- How partnership relationships work.
- The steps from discovery to delivery.
- How Rize tailors a Series or service to the partner.
- What the partner and participants walk away with.
- How learning, websites, and internal systems can be combined in one engagement.

This page should make the offer feel cohesive instead of fragmented.

---

## Learning structure update

The Series-first model should remain the default learning structure.

That means:
- 5 to 8 weekly sessions.
- Cohort-based.
- Modular enough to customize.
- Strong enough to justify larger funding and longer engagements.

You can still keep individual sessions in the background as building blocks, but the page copy should lead with the Series.

---

## Focus areas

Based on your latest direction, the strongest launch priorities are:

1. **Community digital confidence and safety**.
2. **Organizational transformation**.
3. **Career upskilling**.
4. **Entrepreneur support** only if needed.

Creative can stay on the site, but it does not need to be a major early focus unless a partner opportunity appears.

---

## Site logic

A visitor should be able to land on any of the core audience pages and immediately understand:

- What kind of organization this is for.
- What learning Series are available.
- What service support is available.
- What the engagement process looks like.
- What they will walk away with.

That means the site is no longer organized by offer type first. It is organized by **who the work is for**, then by the mix of Series and services that best supports that group.

## Best overall framing

The simplest and strongest frame is:

**Rize helps organizations bridge the information gap, implement practical systems, and train people to use them well.**

Learning Series provide the transformation.
Websites and internal tooling make that transformation usable.
How It Works explains the partnership.
The audience pages tie everything together.
