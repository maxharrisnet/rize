# Rize Skill Training — Website Master Plan

This document is the current source of truth for the website structure and page-direction decisions for **weallrize.ca**.

It consolidates the approved direction from the current copy deck, site framework notes, and the recent restructuring decisions into one working document for planning, content development, and page production.

## Canonical sitemap

The current top-level site structure is:

- Home
- Learning
- Services
- Partnerships
- About

This structure keeps the navigation simple while separating training, implementation services, partnership delivery, and brand/story content into clearer buckets.

### 2. Learning
**Purpose:** Present Rize's education offer through the approved CCC structure.

**Primary job:**
- Organize learning by audience and outcome.
- Make it easy for visitors and partners to identify the best-fit learning path.
- Show both short-format and deeper learning options.

**Approved structure:**
- Hero
- How learning works
- Community
- Career
- Creative
- Looking for something else?

**Positioning notes:**
- This page is now organized by **Community, Career, and Creative** rather than only by tracks.
- It remains the main home for workshops, series, and custom learning.
- Community should lead because it is one of the strongest launch priorities.
- Career should remain learning-centered and avoid drifting into broad services language.
- Creative can stay lean but should remain real and visible.

**Content mapping:**
- **Community:** Digital Confidence, Stay Safe Online, Organizations That Move People
- **Career:** Future-Proof Your Career, modular career-ready sessions
- **Creative:** Creative Work, Amplified, future modular creative-sector offerings
- **How learning works:** workshops, series, delivery options, custom engagements, funded-fit language

**Recommended anchor nav:**
- Community
- Career
- Creative
- How Learning Works

**Status:** Rewritten structure approved. Needs to be turned into full persistent page copy.

**Approved structure:**

- Websites
- Internal systems
- Grant support
- Who this is for
- Closing CTA

**Positioning notes:**
- This page is no longer organized primarily as Organizations vs. Businesses.
- The two core service pillars are:
  - **Websites**

- The page should frame services as helping organizations move from information to implementation.
- Community and nonprofit use cases should remain the strongest emphasis.

**Content mapping:**
- **Websites:** community and nonprofit websites, events and programming sites, lighter creative/service-based website language
- **Internal systems:** Google Workspace cleanup, communication workflows, donation and CRM workflows, reporting and follow-up systems, automation support
- **Grant support:** funding identification, application support, program design language, reporting templates

**Recommended anchor nav:**
- Websites
- Internal Systems
- Grant Support

## Strategic model

The website now follows a cleaner hybrid model:

- **Home** introduces the organization and routes visitors.
- **Learning** houses the training offer using the CCC structure.
- **Services** houses implementation support.
- **Partnerships** explains how collaboration and funded delivery work.
- **About** builds trust and provides story/context/contact.

This keeps the navigation simpler than a fully audience-split site while still applying the strongest audience-based logic where it matters most.

---

## Key messaging principles

### 1. Technology is a means, not the point
Rize is not positioned as selling technology for its own sake. The messaging should keep reinforcing that technology is useful only when it helps people, organizations, and communities do more of what matters.

### 2. Community leads
Community digital confidence, digital safety, and organizational transformation remain the strongest launch focus. Where prioritization is needed, community-facing work should lead.

### 3. Services support implementation
Services should support the learning offer rather than compete with it. Websites and internal systems make the transformation practical and durable.

### 4. Partnerships are trust-based
The partnership model works because organizations already hold trust with their communities. Rize's role is to bring clear, practical, respectful technology education and related support into those spaces.

### 5. Keep CTA language strong and specific
Preferred CTA language includes:
- Start Here
- Get Started
- Talk About a Partnership
- Book a Discovery Call
- Explore Community
- Explore Career
- Explore Creative
