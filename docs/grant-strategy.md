## The model: funded partnerships

We help community organizations bring Rize-delivered digital-skills/AI training to the people they
serve, funded by grants — often at little or no cost to the org.

> **You bring the community. We bring the program, the facilitation, and the funding strategy — so the
> cost lands on a grant, not on the budget.**

- The **partner organization** usually holds/applies for the grant (most programs fund nonprofits, not
  private trainers). Rize designs and delivers the program and assists with the application.
- Some programs fit **Rize directly** or **individuals** (e.g. youth wage-subsidy programs, artist PD
  grants) — tagged "who applies" in the tracker.

## Grant-writing assistance (a named service)

Often what makes a funded partnership possible:
- Funding source identification for the organization's type and community
- Application support and co-writing
- Program-design documentation for grant submissions
- Reporting templates for funded programs

## Funding by segment (how the site frames it, generally)

- **Seniors** — federal/community funding for digital-confidence and online-safety programs.
- **Youth** — federal/provincial youth programs for digital skills, employment readiness, creative-tech.
- **Job seekers & workforce** — youth/employment programs funding digital & AI skills.
- **Indigenous communities** — skills/employment funding delivered through local Indigenous orgs.
- **Black-led organizations** — dedicated capacity-building funding.
- **Community organizations & nonprofits** — provincial, foundation, and corporate community grants.

Reassurance line used on site: *"Programs change constantly — we track what's currently open and match
it to your community."*

## Strategic notes / gaps

- **Strongest fits:** seniors; Black communities & entrepreneurs; workforce/youth (DS4Y); Indigenous
  (via ISET); and the corporate + BC-foundation engines (CIBC, TD, Vancouver Foundation, BC Gaming).
- **Weak fit — creatives:** arts funders pay for *art*, not third-party training delivery. SOCAN PDA and
  FACTOR are the only real levers, and small. No confirmed program pays a creative-sector org to bring
  in an outside trainer for AI/digital upskilling.
- **Headwind — newcomers:** IRCC settlement funding (LINC/IPALS) is being **cut** in BC. Treat newcomer
  funding as a risk, not a lead.
- **Excluded:** StrongerBC Future Skills Grant requires public post-secondary delivery — excludes Rize as
  a private trainer (only viable via a public-institution partnership).
- **Time-sensitive** items (Vancouver Foundation, New Horizons) recur annually — track deadlines yearly.
