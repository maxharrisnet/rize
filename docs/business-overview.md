## What it is

Rize Skill Training is a Vancouver-based technology-education and systems organization. It helps people
and organizations **close the technology gap** — the distance between what today's tools (especially AI)
make possible and what busy people actually have time to figure out. Rize filters the noise, teaches the
skills people actually need, and builds the systems that give them their time back.

- **Name:** Rize Skill Training (rebranded from the former "Rize Digital").
- **Web:** weallrize.ca · **Email:** holler@weallrize.ca
- **Based in:** East Vancouver, serving Vancouver and the Greater Vancouver area.

## Who runs it

Founded by **Max Harris** — an AI specialist and educator with 20+ years building digital systems for
technology companies across North America (early-stage startups to enterprise SaaS). Rooted in East
Vancouver's music, arts, and advocacy communities, with deep Indigenous community ties. Teaching became
as central to the work as building — especially with people who had the most to gain and the least
access to support.

## What it does (two arms)

1. **Learn** — workshops and courses that build the specific tech/AI skills people need (digital
   confidence, online safety, career/AI upskilling; multi-session courses for entrepreneurs, orgs,
   creatives). Delivered publicly, in partnership, or as custom engagements.
2. **Systems** (Build & Automate) — websites clients own, AI tools (chatbots, AI-assisted work),
   automation of repetitive admin/marketing, and integrations (Slack, HubSpot, Google Workspace).
   Sold as outcome-oriented transformation packages plus à-la-carte service categories.

Both arms exist to close the technology gap and reinforce each other.

## Who it serves

Community organizations & nonprofits; small businesses & entrepreneurs; and underserved communities —
seniors, newcomers, Indigenous communities, Black communities, creatives — plus individuals building
confidence. The focus is people who have the most to gain from technology and historically the least
access to it.

## How it makes money

- **Training** — paid workshops and courses, frequently **grant-funded** through community-organization
  partnerships (often at little/no cost to the partner). Grant-writing assistance is a supporting
  service. See [`grant-strategy.md`](grant-strategy.md).
- **Systems projects** — website builds and automation/integration work, priced via website tiers
  (Starter/Standard/Growth + add-ons) and transformation packages (discovery-call scoped).

## How it works / values

Human-first (technology is the means; people, confidence, and impact are the ends); culturally
sensitive (programs and builds shaped around each community); diversity as a core belief (technology
should be for everyone); honest guides, not salespeople (no hype, no fear). Rize operates on the unceded
territories of the Musqueam, Squamish, and Tsleil-Waututh Nations and is committed to serving Indigenous
communities and to reconciliation in digital education.
