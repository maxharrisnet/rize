## One-liner

Rize helps people and organizations close the technology gap — figuring out the tools and skills
they *actually* need, and building the systems that free up their time for the work only they can do.

## The problem we solve

There is a **technology gap** — the distance between what today's tools make possible and what
people are actually able to use. That gap is **wider for the people with the least support**:
nonprofits, small businesses, and underserved communities. The pace of change (especially AI) and
the noise around it make it worse. People don't need more hype; they need someone to **filter the
noise** and help them figure out what's real, what's relevant, and what to skip.

## Who we serve

- **Community organizations & nonprofits** — often stretched thin, doing high-impact work with
  limited capacity.
- **Small businesses & entrepreneurs** — building something, wearing every hat.
- **Underserved communities** — seniors, newcomers, Indigenous communities, Black communities,
  creatives — who have the most to gain and historically the least access.
- **Individuals** — anyone building confidence and skills with technology.

## Value proposition

We deliver **transformation, not tasks**. The through-line across everything:

1. **Efficiency** — eliminate the annoying, repetitive work required to run an organization.
2. **New opportunities** — put capabilities in reach that used to be out of reach.
3. **High impact** — turn saved time and new capability into more reach, more service, more mission.

The promise: **we filter through the noise to help you figure out what tools and skills you
actually need for your work, and we liberate work hours so you can do what you do best.**

## How we do it (principles)

- **Human-first.** Technology is the means; people, confidence, and impact are the ends.
- **Cultural sensitivity.** Programs and builds are shaped around each community's values, language,
  and lived experience.
- **Diversity is key — technology should be for everyone.** Access is the point.
- **Honest guides, not salespeople.** We help people make grounded decisions about what to use and
  what to skip. We don't hype and we don't amplify fear.

## What we offer (two connected arms)

Both arms exist to close the technology gap. They reinforce each other — we teach people to use
tools, and we build the systems those tools power.

### 1. Learn — closing the technology gap
Workshops and courses that build the specific skills people actually need. The **technology gap** is
the explicit throughline here: we make modern tools approachable, cut through the noise, and leave
people more capable and confident. (Currently: Digital Confidence, Stay Safe Online, Future-Proof
Your Career, AI & Modern Workflows; multi-session courses for entrepreneurs, organizations, creatives.)

### 2. Build & Automate — transformative systems
We don't just build sites; we build **systems that make organizations run better**:

- **CMS websites (WordPress) the client can manage independently** — no developer dependency.
- **AI tools** — chatbots, AI-assisted content and research, put to practical use.
- **Automation** — social media posting, email, and marketing/outreach automated so organizations
  serve their audiences better with the same team.
- **Integrations** — connect the tools they already use (Slack, HubSpot, and others) so information
  flows and work happens without manual triggers.
- **Eliminate the annoying work** — the repetitive admin and coordination that eats capacity.
- **Robust, owned systems** — reliable, maintainable, and built for the client to own, not to keep
  them dependent on us.

## Direction: transformative packages — decisions (2026-07-03)

We are moving the offering toward **outcome-oriented transformation packages**. Decided:

- **Packaging model: packages lead, categories stay.** 2–3 named, outcome-oriented packages
  headline the offering page — each bundles site + automation + AI + integrations + training and
  carries a before/after story. The existing service categories remain below as detail for buyers
  who already know what they want. Website pricing tiers (Starter/Standard/Growth) stay attached
  to the website category, not the packages.
- **Naming: the offering is called "SYSTEMS"** (nav label and page identity), replacing
  "Services." Emphasizes robust, owned systems over one-off sites. Pairs with LEARN.
- **Proof: illustrative before/after scenarios.** Until we have real case studies, use vivid,
  honest "what this looks like" vignettes (e.g., a nonprofit coordinator's week before and after
  automation), clearly framed as examples — never fake testimonials. Swap in real case studies as
  projects complete.
- **Scope: sitewide narrative in one pass.** The technology-gap story and value-prop copy roll
  out across Home, Systems, Learn, About, and Partnerships together, so the message is consistent
  everywhere — including the Learning pages, where closing the technology gap is an explicit
  throughline.

## Messaging & voice (for the website)

- Lead with the **value proposition** — pull the viewer into *why this is for them* and *what they'll
  get out of it*, before the "what we do" details.
- **Tell the story.** Think outside the box; make the technology-gap problem and the transformation
  vivid and human.
- Keep the V4 tone rules: positive framing, name communities specifically, outcomes-led, no
  "empower/leverage/solutions," people-first.
- The **technology gap** message should show up on the Learning pages, not just Services.

### Shipped 2026-07-03 (see `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-03-systems-transformation-packages-design.md`)

- **Three packages, live on `/systems`:** OWN YOUR WEBSITE (site the team manages independently),
  GET YOUR TIME BACK (automation + integrations), REACH MORE PEOPLE (chatbot + outreach + AI

- **Package pricing display:** no listed price — every package starts with a free discovery call.
